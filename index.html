website as… tides
The website moves. It expands and contracts depending on the container that holds it. Like water responding to gravity, it reshapes itself to fit the shore.
Content drifts. Words separate as space increases. Meaning feels farther apart. On smaller screens, everything crowds — like shells revealed at low tide.
There is rhythm. The user resizes the window and the page breathes. The margins swell. The letters stretch. Nothing is fixed.
High tide reveals less. Low tide reveals more. The same material exists — but our access changes with the environment.
